# **How to be a Humble Designer without losing your soul** 

## **My approach / aspiration** 
Work like a scientist, present like a snake charmer. 

## **My style**
Generalist vs. specialist
- Labelling myself as a designer to have a starting reference point for people
	- Sometimes helpful, sometimes a burden as their representation of a designer is extremely different
	- I think of design as idea and intention, as a means of understanding the world, not as form/image 
- My thinking and understanding/learning is multi-directional (all the synapses in my brain feel like they are going off at the same time when I'm able to connect the dots. I don’t have lightbulb moments, I have xmas tree lights moments)
	- Thrive within complexity (almost fascinated by it), but don't do good when working under pressure as a norm, as I always need to be switching between open/closed modes of thinking
	- Need to be able to pick up a purpose (not just a plan) to buy into any approach / solution / suggestion
	- It can also slow me down, like getting lost in the details, that's why I need playmates (whom i trust) complementary to me to call !<a href="https://designobserver.com/feature/on-design-bullshit/3347" rel="noopener" class="external-link" target="_blank"><u>bullshit</u></a>! when I derail into the quintessential idealist / magical realism too much
	- Very much driven by emotion, even my logic and reasoning is intuitive. 
		- What this also means is that my mood can have a significant effect on my productivity and ability to think (when I'm in a bad mood, I'm stupid)
		- you'll often hear my arguments start with "I feel like[ …]", but I try to identify what triggers the instinct / feeling with the intention of getting to the essence of it and identify the assumptions I'm making that need to be challenged
- Also an introvert (if we're talking about bad labels and boxes to put people in, but missing a better starting point) 
	- which means I'm going for being in the shadow rather than the spotlight, 
	- observing deeply until speaking up, 
	- put in the effort to get to know people and their mental models to try to speak in their tongue (don't expect people's trust, but rather work to earn it) 
	- easily overstimulated by too much interaction with people - need my quiet time to recharge and internalise, very adamant about my worldview and at the same time inspired to adjust it when discovering more and more from the art of being human
- Old school way back to Ancient Greece style… in thinking that a well-rounded person is multi-disciplinary
	- That comes with a loooot of challenges in the information overload of our era, so focus is essential to get the right/helpful things in the mix
	- I draw inspiration and connections from very unexpected places (that's why I also wanted to join the Dev forum:)
	- I love art and think that it saved me from being normal (a thing of beauty is a joy forever)
	- Naive in the sense that I still believe in searching for truth as a worthwhile pursuit (this for me is the venn diagram intersection between data and design)
## **My belief** 
Communication is the biggest challenge for humans to co-live, co-create, collaborate (people have been taught different things, are they talking about the same thing in a different way?)
- Aim to go for over communication, otherwise chaos subtly instils (the inmates are running the asylum) 
- Asynchronous alignment as much as possible, while the medium doesn't stop the meaning from being delivered (which is the case with customer calls, where being in the moment and observing cannot be replaced by anything else)
- No such thing as a dumb question, no questions are bad/wrong (language was invented to ask questions. answers may be given by grunts or gestures, but questions must be spoken)
- If communication is streamlined (i.e there's a skilled sailor that can handle the winds), we'll be rowing at the same boat
- I don’t believe in hierarchical relationships. I don’t want to be above anybody, and I don’t want to be below anybody. If I can’t treat someone like a peer and if they can’t treat me like a peer, I just don’t want to interact with them.

## **How I view success**
What does being good at my job mean to me? 
- If you have to explain it, you’ve failed 
- Constantly learning and getting better, while having fun (and the fun part is essential to keep at it and have motivation, which is one thing that can't be faked)
- Finding new peepholes to look at the world through - the excitement of an idea that forms inside my head, seeing the possible ways of doing it and then figuring out the steps to make it real
What are the values that underpin my understanding of success?
- It's better to set ambitious goals that we don't always meet, than to sell ourselves short. We can always do and be better.
- People might forget a name, but they'll always remember how you made them feel (hopefully like superheroes)

## **How I communicate**
How have other people described my communication style? 
- Always pushed by others to share more – ideas / perspectives / observations / reflections, but mostly reserved to do so.
What have I gotten feedback about in the past? 
- I don't always find the right time to present/sell my ideas. I noticed this myself and it's keeping me back from sharing most of the time
- This is also caused by me shying away from vulnerability, being a perfectionist by ~~nature~~ (fear) and biassed to contribute to finding a solution rather than adding more questions / uncertainties to the problem at hand
How should others interpret what I do or say?
- I try to be consistent in what I talk about and how I behave, but I'm flawed and it's very valuable to me if people keep me accountable for that
- When I express ideas or observations I can sound very categoric, but most of the time I'm not. I love a good "fight" to a better outcome.
- I will rarely get stuck in one position or opinion but the downside is that you won’t always get a quick judgement out of me—I need to talk it through and see something concrete to get a better feel. Due to this bias, I can sometimes be slow to make a choice and if you need something quickly from me, make sure I know it.
What do I struggle to express? 
- Phasing out the high-level with the low-level detail and being clear about what is context that I consider valuable for people to know, compared with low-level detail that is important now to be able to take action
- Synthesise the multiple pieces of information and detail that I keep in my head when thinking about a topic, to enable fast delivery and understanding to other people
How do I like to stay in sync with others (email, chat, in-person)? 
- If it’s urgent/important/timely or super short, feel free to ping any time, even when I am “red.” I prefer to be involved when things are happening, especially when you need my input or opinion on ongoing work.
- Short questions on ping are fine but I might be inconsistent in response times since I could be in a closed-mode, in the tunnel tackling some task.
- If it’s a long topic and not time sensitive, just try to plan a time to sync.
What’s my availability outside of work hours? 
- I view my work as a way to get to know the world and influence it. It might be that this brings about an unhealthy relationship with work (for my lack of clear boundaries). But because most of it happens through thinking, I can't switch off and stick to a set schedule.
- I try to make it clear when I'm completely offline and why, but I'm uncomfortable when I don't know if I can be fully transparent and upfront and I have doubts on how it would impact others.
- Usually, I stretch my work days and don't mind going longer than normal working hours. But, this comes with the downside that at some point I get too tired and I need to recharge.

## **Things I do that may annoy you**
What’s the cause of misunderstandings that I've had in the past? 
- Asking too many questions
- Being a stickler for the details (I honestly believe the devil is in the details)
- Taking too long to act, spending too much time analysing / overthinking a situation / problem / approach
What are some things about my style that other people have given me critical feedback on? 
- That I have a low voice, which makes it hard for me to to get my point across and be heard
What quirks or mannerisms might unintentionally annoy a different personality type?  
- Needing time and space to form thoughts of ideas on my own terms
- Not sharing before my ideas are fully formed and then getting frustrated there's no more time to take a good approach
- Shutting off when I get frustrated with my inability to communicate what's in my head
- Slow to make a choice or decision based only on imagination. I need options, things to play with and protected time to think through
- Hard to get me to do something I don't understand the purpose of, if I do it it's always going to show through the output
- When I lose purpose and motivation, the quality of my outputs decreases dramatically
- Can sound sane, but don't get fooled by it. I can mislead you at first, but sooner or later the crazy will come out.

Curious to get feedback from the team! What do I do that's annoying to you?

## **What gains and loses my trust**
What actions can a person take to gain my trust? 
- Active listening
- Caring and offering support
- Arguments around their way of thinking or approach
Conversely, what triggers me? 
- Very much triggered by smoking mirrors. Rhetoric that is just fluff, meant to make people's heads spin and end in agreeing.
- Can't stand selfishness! Even though it's very human (I also struggle with it) I get a visceral reaction to it when I see it repeatedly and to people that are not self-aware.

## **My strengths**
What do I love to do and are good at? 
- Thinking about systems and spotting the interconnections between things in the world
- Crafting stories to connect with people. Better at visual storytelling than writing stories
What can I help others with?
- Asking questions to facilitate more than one perspective
- Finding beauty and delight in the smallest details
- Listening

## **My growth areas**
What are my blind spots? 
- Being biassed to go with the flow to be able to get to know someone's style and approach, without trusting that I need to challenge them the same way I hope for them to challenge me.
- Doubting my process and finding excuses
- Overthinking, which I believe has a paralysing effect and hinders me from taking action at the right time
What am I working on (to improve)? 
- My thinking through writing
- My communication style, being present in active discussions, to be able to make better use of the moment
- My finesse - my ability to handle sensitive dynamics when I notice what’s unsaid
	- Read a situation
	- Understand my levers are
	- Identify risks
	- Determine what to do or say
	- Determine how to do it or say it
	- Execute my strategy to get the outcome I’m looking for
What can others help me with?
- Constant, honest feedback (even if it might seem it's brutal) and calling bullshit on me!
- Contribute with their ideas and thinking to the work I do, even if it feels like overstepping - it's not!! I like collaborating, improv and building off each other's ideas. I hate feeling an expectation to have all the needed answers or a final say and thrive when feeling we're figuring things out together.

## **How I give and receive feedback**
What is my philosophy around feedback? 
- Did my feedback result in the other person taking action in a way I and they feel good about?
What can others expect in receiving feedback from me? 
- Too much 😂
How would I prefer to receive feedback from others?
- Make it actionable – that doesn't mean give me a solution and step by step instructions on how to do a change, but rather make me understand where you're coming from clearly
- Most useful – when trying something out, record yourself thinking out loud and interacting with it. Seeing your experience first hand connects the dots better for me than if there's a filtering layer applied (I see unparalleled value in learning directly from the source, free of intermediaries)
- Have a bit of patience – help me navigate the questions that pop into my head when hearing your feedback. This provides me with reassurance that we are on the same page and the action I take based on your feedback is also aligned with your expectations.
